# What to eat during RT (prostate radiotherapy)

**Original question:** Starting 20 fractions of RT. Advice for 2–3 days before and during treatment? Confused between maintaining fibre vs low-fibre bland meals.

## Consensus from patients
- **Avoid gas/wind** during treatment — positioning matters
- **Cut out:** alcohol, fizzy drinks, caffeine, coffee
- **Avoid high-fibre / gas-producing foods:** broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, beans, pulses, green leafy vegetables
- **Low-fibre bland meals** — opposite of "healthy eating" but necessary for RT positioning
- **Peppermint tea** can help (some patients grew to dislike it)
- **Non-dairy milk with cereal** mentioned
- Walk and pass wind once before fraction if needed
- Hospital should provide **dietary guide** — call oncology nurses if not received
- Example leaflet: [UHCW prostate RT dietary info (PDF)](https://www.uhcw.nhs.uk/download/clientfiles/files/Patient%20Information%20Leaflets/Medicine/Oncology/Radiotherapy%20information%20for%20patients%20undergoing%20treatment%20to%20the%20prostate%20or%20prostate%20bed.pdf)

## Relevance for assistant
- Diet advice **depends on treatment type** — pelvic RT ≠ chemo nausea advice
- Symptom tag: `radiotherapy` + `gas/bloating` → low fibre, not high fibre

## Patient language patterns
- "confused between fibre and low fibre"
- "what to eat during RT"
- "gas/wind is the enemy"
