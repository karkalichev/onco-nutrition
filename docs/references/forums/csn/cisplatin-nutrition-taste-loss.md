# Maintaining nutrition during cisplatin therapy

**Original question:** What did anyone do to maintain adequate nutrition when you had cisplatin therapy and loss of taste? Boyfriend may undergo it again post-surgery. Radiation killed taste buds first time; cisplatin made everything taste like metal. Hoping to avoid another g-tube.

## Solutions from patients

### Commercial supplements
- **Ensure / Boost / Boost VHC** (530 cal per 8 oz) — sip all day
- **Ensure in feeding tube bag + water** for hydration + nutrition
- **Benecalorie** (Nestle) — 330 cal, 7g protein per small container; buttery, mixes into foods
- **Carnation Instant Breakfast**, protein powder, full-fat ice cream in shakes
- **Soylent** as meal replacement alternative

### High-calorie shake recipe (~1300 cal, sip all day)
- Coffee cream 18% — 1 cup (480 cal)
- Coconut milk — 1/3 cup (150 cal)
- Greek yogurt — 1 cup (270 cal)
- Ground flax — 2 tbsp (100 cal)
- Hemp hearts — 3 tbsp (170 cal)
- Nut butter — 1 tsp (100 cal)
- Honey — 1 tsp (100 cal)
- Banana — 80 cal

### Pudding recipe (4 servings, weight gain)
- 1 box instant pudding (3.4 oz)
- 3/4 cup half & half
- 1/2 cup water
- 1 cup Carnation evaporated milk (canned)
- Mix 1 minute until thick

### Practical tips
- **Smoothies easier than chewing** when taste is gone — can chug 20 oz without swallowing issues
- Add **cream or Greek yogurt** to everything; flax/hemp seeds to soups and cereal
- **Mashed potatoes** with heavy cream and gravy
- **Soft eggs, oatmeal, jello, pudding, ice cream** slide down easily
- **Split pea soup, baked beans with honey** for calories
- Keep **eating/drinking** to maintain esophagus function even with g-tube
- Track what smells/tastes worse — partner kept notes and remade smoothies
- **Peanut butter, oil, ghee** in smoothies for calories
