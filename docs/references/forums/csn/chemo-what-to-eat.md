# Chemo, what to eat and what not to eat?

**Original question:** Being the research guy I am, I've checked out a lot of sites regarding food and chemo. I'll be taking pills by the way. Can any of you pass on some of your food/drink experiences? Good and bad!

## Key peer responses (summarized)

### Practical consensus
- **Check with oncology nutritionist** at your hospital
- **Drink plenty of water**; eat what sounds good when sick
- **Don't take supplements** without doctor approval — some interfere with treatment
- **Avoid high antioxidants** during chemo/rads (green tea, blueberries, pomegranate) — may counteract oxidizing effect of chemo (MSK integrative med)
- **Avoid grapefruit** — interacts with many medications
- **Cold sensitivity:** water without ice during chemo
- **Taste changes:** bland food works (oats + milk + honey); adding spice can help when things don't taste right
- **Food diary** helps identify what you tolerate
- **Cravings** — body may want comfort foods; eat them
- **When nauseous:** toast, porridge, salmon + rice + pickled ginger for bad taste in mouth; warm (not cold) foods

### Conflicting advice (important for assistant to handle)
- **"Eat whatever you want"** vs **evidence-based low-carb/keto/fasting** — heated debate in thread
- One member: 20 years cancer-free on oats, fruit, fish only
- Another: pesco-vegetarian ideal but **keeping weight up is priority**
- Keto/fasting proponents cite Longo, Seyfried — others push back: "lack of evidence"
- **Curcumin/turmeric** — conflicting (avoid with some regimens vs. research support)
- **Probiotics** — some recommend, others say avoid during chemo

### Representative quotes
> "I just let my stomach choose… I survived for a year on rolled oats made on milk with a dollop of honey."

> "Eat what you can tolerate. The chemo changes everything — the bottom line is eat something."

> "My doctor told me to drink plenty of fluids and if you get sick try to eat what sounds good."

> "Please don't take anything at all without checking with your team."
